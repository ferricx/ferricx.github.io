Accessibility

What is Accessibility?

Accessibility means designing and building things so that everyone can participate in activities — regardless of ability, disability, age, situation, or context.

It applies everywhere: physical spaces, products, services, and digital experiences. An accessible ramp, a clearly worded document, a readable sign, and a keyboard-navigable app are all examples of accessibility in practice.

The goal is to remove barriers so that everyone can participate, independently and on equal terms.

What kinds of activities?
  • Eating
  • Travelling
  • Using the bathroom
  • Working
  • Making purchases
  • Enjoying a live show
  • Enjoying an activity

Accessible Development

Accessible development is the practice of building digital user experiences that everyone can use autonomously, with as little friction as possible.

This means writing code that works across different devices, input methods, and assistive technologies — so that no one needs help or workarounds to use what you build.

Who is everyone?

All people regardless of age, ability, disability, language, device, connection speed, or level of technical experience. This includes people who use screen readers, keyboard-only navigation, voice control, or switch devices — as well as people on slow connections, small screens, or unfamiliar platforms.
